Your net words-per-minute (WPM) takes your gross WPM and adjusts it based on your accuracy.

Since words can vary in length, a "word" in this case is defined as a distinct block of 5 characters (including spaces). Therefore, the number of words typed is defined as the total number of typed characters divided by 5.

To get your net WPM (the displayed metric), simply divide the total number of characters typed by 5, then normalise for 1 minute and multiply by the accuracy.

E.g. 500 characters in 2 minutes (120 seconds), of which 18 were incorrect, will result in an accuracy of 96.4%.
500 characters equates to 100 words in 2 minutes which normalises to a gross WPM of 50.
Multiply 50 by the accuracy (96.4%) and round to get a final result of 48 WPM.
